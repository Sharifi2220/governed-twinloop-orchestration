# Role model

The pattern defines logical responsibilities, not particular products, identities, or technical privileges. One person or system may support more than one role, but execution and approval should remain visibly distinct.

## Human authority

The human authority owns the purpose, risk posture, and final decision. This role sets scope, resolves escalations, and may stop work at any time. Accountability is not delegated to an agent.

## Supervisor

The supervisor translates human intent into a bounded task, checks the worker report, and recommends or records an outcome: approve, revise, or stop. The supervisor must not treat missing evidence as successful completion.

## Bounded worker

The worker acts only within the task card. It records what it did, distinguishes created output from drafts or proposals, and stops when a prerequisite or gate is reached. It may recommend a next step but does not authorize one.

## Reviewer or specialist

Where needed, a reviewer or domain specialist examines a specific concern such as citation quality, privacy, licensing, or safety. Specialist review informs human judgment; it does not erase the human decision point.

## Illustrative working model

In one possible tool-assisted workflow, **Codex** may serve as supervisor and reviewer while **Hermes** serves as a bounded worker. These are illustrative role assignments, not requirements or claims of a technical connection. If the worker is unavailable, a human may transfer a task card and return the worker report for review.

## Separation rule

A worker report is evidence for review, not approval. A supervisor recommendation is not automatic authorization. The human authority retains the right to accept, revise, reject, or terminate the work.
